## 5. Role Analysis

### True seniority and scope
Director of Design for the Portfolios product area — a domain lead, not the head of the overall design org. This is a mid-senior leadership role: likely managing a team of 4–8 product and content designers focused on the managed investing product. Seniority is real; scope is domain-bounded.

### Reporting line and team context
Not stated in the JD. *Inference:* Likely reports to a VP of Design or Head of Design. The presence of multiple Director-level design roles (Director Product Design, Director Design Portfolios, Design Director Brand) signals a larger design org with domain-based structure. Design does not appear to report directly to the CEO — more likely to CPO or VP Design.

### Responsibilities and expected outcomes
- Lead product and content designers across the Portfolios experience
- Define and execute design strategy for the managed investing product
- Partner with Product, Engineering, and Portfolio Managers
- Elevate UX and visual standards across the Portfolios surface area
- Mentor and scale the design team
- Drive experimentation using research and client data

### Strategic influence vs. production focus
Leans strategic within its domain. "Bringing clarity to complexity" and "shaping core business strategy" language suggests meaningful strategic ownership. But scope is the Portfolios area, not company-wide — the strategic influence is real but bounded.

### Required skills and recurring market patterns
JD emphasises: design team leadership at scale, fintech/regulated industry experience (bonus), strong interaction and visual design, cross-functional alignment, and comfort with ambiguity. Fintech experience preferred but not mandatory. AI integration mentioned as company-wide expectation ("lean into new technologies including AI"). Pattern is consistent with current Director-level JDs in Canadian product companies.
